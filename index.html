I am a researcher specialising in cardiovascular pharmacology, proteomics, and pharmacogenomics. My work bridges translational science and clinical practice — from uncovering blood-based biomarkers for heart failure subtypes to investigating epigenetic regulation in diabetic cardiomyopathy. I am committed to developing precision medicine strategies that improve patient outcomes across Thailand and beyond.

Work in Progress

Research
Projects

01
Biomarker Discovery for Heart Failure Subtypes
Untargeted proteomics to identify blood-based biomarkers distinguishing HFpEF from HFrEF in at-risk individuals — improving early diagnosis beyond BNP measurements.
◎ Completed · Published 2025
02
Epigenetic Regulation in Diabetic Cardiomyopathy
Comprehensive investigation of DNA methylation, histone modifications, and epitranscriptomics in the initiation and progression of diabetic cardiomyopathy (DCM).
● Active · 2024–Present
03
Diabetic Cardiomyopathy Mouse Model
Deep phenotyping of a modified preclinical DCM model that more faithfully reflects clinical disease progression, enabling mechanistic studies and therapeutic evaluation.
◎ Completed · Published 2025
04
Sacubitril/Valsartan in DCM
Investigating the immunomodulatory properties of sacubitril/valsartan and its potential to attenuate progression of diabetic cardiomyopathy.
◎ Completed · Published 2025
05
Pharmacogenomics in Thai Hospital Pharmacists
Cross-sectional survey evaluating knowledge, attitude, and practice towards pharmacogenomics among 600 pharmacists across 21 hospitals in Thailand.
◎ Completed · Published 2020
06
PGx Implementation in Thai Healthcare
Ongoing effort to facilitate integration of pharmacogenomic knowledge into clinical practice across Thai healthcare settings, focusing on policy development and training.
● Active · Ongoing
